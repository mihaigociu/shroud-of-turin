## Key Findings at a Glance

**Established with high confidence (multiple independent methods, replicated):**
- The image is not paint, dye, or any applied pigment
- Image coloration is confined to the outermost ~200–600 nm of fiber surface
- The image is a photographic negative encoding 3D cloth-to-body distance without geometric distortion
- Blood marks were deposited *before* the image formed
- The blood areas contain real biological material consistent with whole blood (bilirubin, porphyrins, albumin, serum halos)

**Genuinely contested:**
- Whether the 1988 C14 sample was representative of the main cloth (the data show real statistical heterogeneity; the reweave hypothesis is unlikely but not definitively closed)
- The image-formation mechanism — no proposed hypothesis satisfies all constraints, especially **double superficiality** (a faint reverse-face image discovered after the 2002 restoration)
- The pollen/botanical evidence (Frei's work is methodologically compromised; only 3 species independently confirmed)

**Not supported by the evidence:**
- McCrone's iron-oxide paint conclusion (contradicted by STURP's protease tests)
- Garlaschelli/Nickell reproductions as full replications (fail on superficiality depth, resolution, geometry, and blood sequence)
- Fanti/De Caro 1st-century dating as established alternatives to C14 (unvalidated methods, unverified sample provenance)

**The synthesis conclusion:** The Shroud is a genuinely anomalous object — if medieval, made by techniques unknown to materials science; if 1st-century, its image-formation event remains physically inexplicable. Confident conclusions in either direction currently outrun the evidence. The definitive test — a new multi-site radiocarbon study — requires Church authorization that has not been granted.

## Research Approach

Each phase was researched against primary peer-reviewed sources. Claims are assessed on their own evidential merit with explicit attention to:
- Publication venue and peer-review status
- Independence of the researchers from declared advocacy positions
- Whether findings have been independently replicated
- What specific constraints each claim satisfies or fails
