# Shroud of Turin — Systematic Research Plan

> **Goal:** Evaluate all major claims about the Shroud of Turin through a structured, evidence-first lens — covering history, science, theology, and ongoing controversy.

---

## 1. What Is the Shroud of Turin?

**Research questions:**
- Physical description: dimensions, material, condition
- The double full-body image: what it shows and how it appears
- The "blood" marks and their correspondence to crucifixion wounds
- Current location and custodianship (Cathedral of Saint John the Baptist, Turin)

**Sources to consult:**
- STURP (Shroud of Turin Research Project) final summary report (1981)
- Barrie Schwortz's shroud.com archive
- Vatican / Diocese of Turin official statements

## 2. Historical Provenance & Documentary Record

**Research questions:**
- First undisputed historical appearance: Lirey, France, ~1354–1357 (Geoffrey de Charny)
- Earlier alleged records: the Mandylion of Edessa, the Image of Edessa, Byzantine icons
- The "missing centuries" problem: no documented history before 14th c.
- Transfer to House of Savoy (1453) → Turin (1578)
- Key events: 1532 fire damage, 1694 Guarini chapel, 1898 Secondo Pia photograph

**Key claims to evaluate:**
- [ ] Ian Wilson's Mandylion hypothesis (1978) — is the shroud the Edessa cloth folded?
- [ ] Jack Markwardt's Antioch theory
- [ ] The de Clari 1203 Constantinople account of a burial cloth showing Christ's figure

## 3. Radiocarbon Dating (1988)

**What happened:**
- Three independent labs: University of Oxford, University of Arizona, ETH Zurich
- Sample from one corner; dated to **1260–1390 AD** (95% confidence)
- Published in *Nature*, 1989 (Damon et al.)

**Claims challenging the dating:**
- [ ] Contamination hypothesis (Rogers 2005 — vanillin content / kinetics argument)
- [ ] Reweaving/repair patch hypothesis (Sue Benford & Joseph Marino — the "invisible reweave")
- [ ] Bacterial contamination (Leoncio Garza-Valdes, 1993)
- [ ] Statistical heterogeneity argument (Remi Van Haelst, others)
- [ ] Recent counter-analysis: Bryan Walsh's re-examination of the raw data

**Key sources:**
- Damon et al., *Nature* 337 (1989)
- Rogers, *Thermochimica Acta* 425 (2005)
- Benford & Marino papers
- Fanti et al. alternative dating studies (see §5)

---

## 4. The 1978 STURP Investigation

**What STURP was:**
- 40+ American scientists; 120 hours of direct access to the cloth
- Multidisciplinary: spectroscopy, X-ray fluorescence, photomicrography, UV fluorescence, VP-8 image analysis

**Key findings:**
- Image is *not* paint, dye, or photographic emulsion
- Image resides on the very surface of linen fibrils (200–600 nm depth)
- 3D-encoded luminance information (VP-8 relief image)
- Blood marks preceded the image (serum halos visible under UV)
- No directionality to image formation; no brushstrokes

**Open STURP conclusion:** "The image was produced by some interaction of the body and the cloth … the exact mechanism remains unknown."

**Sources:**
- STURP summary (1981), *Analytica Chimica Acta*
- Individual STURP member papers (Heller & Adler on blood; Jackson et al. on 3D)

## 5. Alternative Dating Methods & Recent Physical Studies

**Research questions:**
- What do non-radiocarbon dating methods suggest?

**Studies to evaluate:**
- [ ] Giulio Fanti et al. (2013) — Mechanical (tensile) and chemical (FT-IR, Raman) dating: estimated **300 BC – 400 AD**. Published in *Journal of Imaging Science and Technology*
- [ ] Fanti & Malfi (2015) book: *The Shroud of Turin: First Century after Christ!*
- [ ] De Caro et al. (2022) — Wide-angle X-ray scattering (WAXS) dating: estimated **~first century AD** (*Heritage* journal, MDPI)
- [ ] Liberato De Caro team credibility and methodology critique

## 6. The Image Formation Problem

**Core puzzle:** What physical/chemical process produced the image?

**Hypotheses to evaluate:**

| Hypothesis | Proponent(s) | Key evidence for | Key evidence against |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct contact (sweat/ammonia vapours) | Various | Explains body contact areas | Does not explain 3D encoding or non-contact areas |
| Medieval artistic forgery | Bishop Pierre d'Arcis (1389); sceptics | C14 date; historical gap | STURP findings; no pigment; 3D info |
| Proto-photography (silver + acid) | Nicholas Allen (1993) | Explains negative image | Requires 14th-c. optics; no chemical trace |
| Corona discharge / radiation burst | John Jackson; Fanti | Explains superficiality; 3D | No verified mechanism; exotic |
| Maillard reaction (body vapour + linen) | Rogers (2002) | Chemistry plausible | Can't reproduce image resolution |
| Excimer UV laser (body radiation) | Paolo Di Lazzaro et al., ENEA (2011) | Reproduces superficiality | Requires exotic energy source |

**Key sources:**
- Jackson et al., *Applied Optics* (1984)
- Di Lazzaro et al., *Applied Optics* (2012)
- Rogers, *Thermochimica Acta* (2002)
- Nicholas Allen thesis / book

## 7. Blood & Forensic Evidence

**Research questions:**
- Is the "blood" human blood? What type?
- Do wound patterns match Roman crucifixion practices?
- Scourge marks: number, pattern, instrument type (flagrum)
- Head wounds consistent with a crown of thorns?
- Side wound (spear); wrist vs palm nail wounds

**Key findings to verify:**
- [ ] Heller & Adler (1980): identified blood as whole blood with bilirubin (trauma indicator)
- [ ] Blood type AB (Baima Bollone; contested)
- [ ] Forensic pathologist analyses: Frederick Zugibe vs. Pierre Barbet (nail through wrist debate)
- [ ] Matteo Borrini & Luigi Garlaschelli (2018, *Journal of Forensic Sciences*): blood flow angle inconsistent with crucifixion — **evaluate methodology**

## 8. Pollen & Botanical Evidence

**Research questions:**
- Max Frei's 1973/1978 pollen analysis: claimed pollen from Palestine, Turkey, and France
- Credibility issues: Frei later implicated in Hilter Diaries forgery
- Uri Baruch (Israeli botanist) partial re-examination
- Avinoam Danin (Hebrew University): plant images on shroud match flora of Jerusalem area

**Claims to evaluate:**
- [ ] Frei's pollen results reproducible?
- [ ] Danin's flower image identifications — methodology critique

## 9. Numismatic & Art-Historical Evidence

**Research questions:**

- Iconographic parallels: does Byzantine Christ iconography derive from the shroud face?
- The Pray Codex (Hungary, ~1192–1195): does it depict the shroud?

## 10. Theological & Institutional Positions

**Research questions:**
- Catholic Church's official stance (relic of devotion, not declared authentic)
- Pope John Paul II (1998 ostension), Pope Francis statements
- History of Church-authorized investigations and restrictions
- Eastern Orthodox position
- Protestant positions

## 11. Skeptical & Mainstream Scientific Consensus

**Key sceptical arguments:**
- C14 dating remains the strongest scientific evidence (peer-reviewed, three labs)
- No credible historical record before 14th century
- Image could be produced by medieval techniques (McCrone: iron oxide pigment — contested by STURP)
- Luigi Garlaschelli's 2009 reproduction using medieval techniques

**Sources:**
- Joe Nickell, *Inquest on the Shroud of Turin* (1983, updated 1998)
- Walter McCrone, *Judgment Day for the Shroud* (1999)
- Garlaschelli (2009), *Journal of Imaging Science and Technology*

---

## 12. Key Open Questions & Research Gaps

- Why does the 1988 C14 sample location matter so much, and has it been independently verified as non-representative?
- Can WAXS / mechanical dating methods be validated on cloths of known age?
- Is a definitive new dating study (multiple sample locations) politically feasible?
- What would falsify the authenticity hypothesis? What would falsify the forgery hypothesis?
